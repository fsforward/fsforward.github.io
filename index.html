About Me:

Greetings! I'm a self-taught developer with a passion for coding and technology. My journey in the world of programming has been a fascinating one, filled with learning experiences and creative challenges. Although I'd rather not disclose my name for privacy concerns, I'm excited to share my story with you.

My journey began with a curiosity about web development. I delved into the world of HTML and CSS, crafting my first websites from scratch. While I managed to grasp the essentials, my struggle with front-end development persisted, probably because I tended to dive straight into coding without proper design planning. "fsforward.com," went through several design iterations before settling into its current layout (that I made in 3 days so don't expect it being good).

My love for coding eventually led me towards game development. Unity and C# became my companions on this new adventure. For about two years, I honed my skills in this domain, crafting game mechanics and exploring the nuances of interactive experiences. Unfortunately, my laptop's limitations hindered my progress, and the hunt for cohesive textures proved challenging. Assembling a collection of textures that harmoniously fit together posed a daunting task, ultimately prompting me to step away from Unity and C#.

Adapting to new challenges, I ventured into the realm of modding Minecraft, I was able to play Minecraft with performance mods. So I was able to make some mods myself. Despite the limitations of my hardware, I found joy in this pursuit.
The desire to create games returned , but the texture and performance barriers held me back from reengaging with Unity and C#.

So, I RECENTLY embarked on a journey to learn C++ an Raylib. I chose to work with C++ due to its performance benefits and potential for intricate control. The speed and versatility of C++ captivated me, and I'm committed to honing my skills in this language while setting my sights on more advanced frameworks beyond Raylib.

Beyond programming, I harbor a fascination for ethical hacking. The ever-present vulnerabilities of the internet inspire me to contribute positively by enhancing cybersecurity. However, the vastness of this field has left me uncertain about where to begin, preventing me from fully exploring its potential.

In addition to coding and tech, I have an affinity for Samsung phones and enjoy rooting them. This process empowers me with extended battery life, free in-app purchases, enhanced performance, and more, adding a touch of customization to my digital life.

And there you have it — a glimpse into who I am and my journey in the world of technology and programming. Thank you for taking the time to learn about me!

About me: Understanding My Autism

I would like to provide you with a better understanding of my diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)(Pervasive Developmental Disorder – Not Otherwise Specified/PDD-NOS).

Shutdowns:
I experience shutdowns, which are a response to reaching a crisis point due to high levels of stress(?). These shutdowns are characterized by my withdrawal from the world around me. During a shutdown, I may partially or completely withdraw from communication.

During these shutdowns, if you notice me not responding or staring into the distance, it's important to understand that I'm in a state of withdrawal and not fully present. Avoid attempting to forcefully engage or make contact with me during these times, as my focus and awareness have receded. Trying to interact might only exacerbate the stress and prolong the shutdown. Your understanding and patience during these moments are greatly appreciated.

If a shutdown happens due to a specific topic, please recognize that it's best to avoid pursuing that topic further. If I'm not responding, attempting to bring up the same subject again won't lead to a response. It's a sign that my capacity for engagement on that particular subject is (currently) limited.

Sensory Sensitivities:
I have sensory sensitivities, particularly to bright white light. I struggle with keeping my eyes open in environments with bright, "hospital-like" lights, which can lead to discomfort and even passing out. Similarly, I find it challenging to keep my eyes open in sunlight, even on cloudy days, though I won't pass out from sunlight.

Triggers for Shutdowns:

  • Excessive demands or expectations placed on me, especially when they are not related to each other.
  • Unexpected changes in plans or routines.
  • Sensory overload from bright lights, or crowded environments.
  • Social overload, which occurs when I'm exposed to too much social interaction in a short period.

Communication and my way of Learning:
I avoid eye contact.
I mostly figure things out on my own if I am interested in something.
I prefer to work alone, even when participating in a group setting. Unless I'm with friends, I may be able to work within a group.

Learning can be challenging for me, particularly when I'm not interested in the topic at hand. While this might seem normal for many, due to my autism, it becomes extra difficult as I tend to uncontrollably forget information and lose focus, often resulting in a dismissive "Yea sure, whatever" attitude. I THINK it would help to have information presented to me in alternative ways or through one-on-one sessions if I don't understand something.
